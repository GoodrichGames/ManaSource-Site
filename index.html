Overview

Mana Source is an upcoming competitive living card game (LCG) for 1-4 players. It focuses around player vs player arena-style combat to battle for control of Mana Wells scattered throughout the mysterious world of Kainan. It features five playable primary classes, one exclusively secondary class, and over 300 unique cards in the release set. We are looking at two different release strategies, either a core set or selling by individual classes.

Summary

Experiment, strategize, and mindgame to reduce your opponent's health to zero and win!


Playtime: 20-60 minutes

Players: 1-4

Ages: 8+


GOALS: Fun, quick to play, timed actions, hundreds of skill combinations, replayability, LCG, no/minimal RNG, easy to pick up, hard to master, strategy based, mind games.


Secondary goals: Few components, kid budget friendly.

Tired of RNG deciding your games?

We believe in minimal competitive randomness. No draw step, no shuffling, but no two games are the same.


1. Choose a primary class

2. Choose a secondary class

3. Pick 9 cards from the two classes.

Tired of In-balanced Cards?

Mana Source features a unique balancing system never seen before in a physical card game! Players are encouraged to sign up for push notifications on the website to receive live notifications of balance changes. The full list of latest card versions are available on the Resources page. Each set will include a set of stickers used to change card values for balancing purposes.

How to Play

Each round, a player selects 1 card to play and plays it facedown in the center of the play area. Players simultaneously reveal the cards, pay the card cost, resolve the action, and resolve other effects. Cards enter one of 9 “timer” zones to indicate that it cannot be cast until removed from the timer zones. Cards move to the lower numeric recharge step zone at the end of each round. If a card is in the lowest timer zone, the next round it moves back to the player’s hand.

Explore the Classes

Wizard (Elemancer) - Command over the elements: Fire, Water (Ice), Earth, Electricity, Air. This group of people tends to live as traveling merchants to other nations. They are long sought after for their terraforming, forge supporting, water ducts, farming.


Warden (Ecomancer) - Command over living nature: Plants, Animals, tools/weapons made from plants, nature spirits. This group of people tends to live in “wooded” communities for obvious reasons. Many of the villages are constructed from natural materials on the ground floor of the forest, then raised up to just below canopy level via growth into the tree or controlled vines. The canopy is woven into tightly packed foliage that has the appearance of hills and rolling pastures rather than trees. These merge into the surrounding landscape, creating vast, dark ground areas that many choose to ignore. Some say the dead have washed down below year after year. Other villages live in vast networks of caves in mountains and steep ridges, villages extending far up into the rock. They tend to be curious in nature and explorers. Tend to be bonded with animals though they do hunt and eat animals too wild even for them to tame. They provide a great deal of natural medicine, toxins, herding, and food for other nations.


Illusionist (Illusimancer) - Command over illusions, perception, and the mind. Generally thought of as vagrants, criminals, and untrustworthy by nature. Various involvement in criminal activity such as forgery, disguises, “gambling”, suggestion, and interrogation. As such their numbers have dwindled over the years due to prosecution and persecution. Illusionists that do have claim to legitimacy often live as performers, soothers, councilors, and criminologists, though many are builders - they value reality. They maintain a facade of huge illusionary parapets, grand towers, and other resources to project power as a nation but in reality exists as a few small legitimate living families.


Knight (Vigormancer) - Command over physical aptitude: strength, speed, endurance. An honorable community with a bloody history. Upon a time the majority of the humans in the world were vigormancers, priding themselves on physical prowess and glory in battle above all else. Various wars were fought over land control, power, and a fear of more outward magic users. Many choose to ignore their families’ exploits in the last great war, tracing their lineage only back to a time when the Knight’s code was established. Some now say that the great war never happened at all, that is it a myth made up by opposing nations to vilify the Knight nation-states. They rarely directly channel mana in the same way that the other magic schools do, preferring the strength found in adrenaline coursing through their veins. It is not well known that Vigomancers (more commonly called, Knights) still draw and convert mana in this process but due to enhancements in their physical systems, consume it at a more efficient rate.


Doctor (Biomancer) - Command over life: life force, life <-> mana conversion, automatons, sickness. An all but lost art. Once the doctors and medics of the vigormancer empires, many were forced into creating human mana sources, plagues, and combat automatons that directly opposed their underlying belief in the sanctity of life. “What is made should not be unmade.” Many chose to take on the appearance of death itself instead of serve those who would subvert that. Their automatons roam the land searching for ways to fulfill their defined purpose, unbeholden to any master but the one who created them. They have dwindled much in number and largely become myth, only the automatons evidence that they ever existed at all. Those who have taken on death await an awakening.


Solarmancy - Command over light, courage, shields. Teachers, scholars, and leadership. Their race is generally long dead though many of their gems are used by vigormancer leadership.
